Maintenance operations
Warning
Brake fluid is corrosive and will damage paintwork. Avoid contact with eyes and skin. In case of accidental contact, wash the affected area with abundant running water and consult a doctor if necessary.
 
For all maintenance operations (wear check and replacement of brake pads and brake fluids, etc.) see Sect. D 4, Checking brake pad wear and replacing brake pads, Changing the brake fluid.
Removing the front brake system
 
Operations

Drain the braking system
D 4, Changing the brake fluid
Undo the special screw (1) securing the brake hose to the master cylinder and recover the sealing washers (2).
Undo the screw (21) and slide the front brake hose (3) from the support (4) on the bottom yoke.
Undo the screw (23) and remove the front brake hose (3) from the hose clip (20).
 
Warning
If you damage the hose clip (20) upon removal of the front brake hose, you shall replace it (Sect. E 4, Removing the front mudguard).
If hose is not fastened by hose clip, it might interfere with tyre under braking and provoke accidents.
 
Loosen the two retaining screws (9) securing the left front brake calliper (11) to the fork leg.
Repeat the operation for the right brake calliper (10).
Keep the spacers (8).
Undo the special screws (7) from both callipers and recover the sealing washers (2).
Detach the front brake callipers from hoses (3) and (15).
Removing the brake discs
The front brake discs consist of an inner carrier, which is mounted to the wheel, and an outer rotor. Both parts must be changed together as a pair.
 
Remove the front wheel (Sect. G 1, Removing the front wheel).
Undo the screws (14) securing the brake discs to the wheel and remove the disc (13) from both sides of the wheel.
Overhauling the front brake components
Important
Critical safety components. The brake calliper manufacturer recommends you not to attempt to service the internal components of the brake callipers. Incorrect overhaul of this component could endanger rider safety.
 
Operations should be limited to replacement of the pads, fasteners and the bleed valve assembly.
Refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this section for indications on replacement of the above components.
The brake disc must be clean, without any signs of rust, oil, grease or dirt and no deep scoring.
To check the wear of the brake discs follow the data detailed in Sect. C 1.1, Hydraulic brakes.
Refitting the brake discs
Before refitting the brake disc to the wheel, clean all contact surfaces thoroughly and smear a medium strength threadlocker on the threads of retaining screws (14).
Tighten the fixing screws (14) of the brake disc (13) to the wheel following this sequence: 1-3-5-2-4.
Tighten the screws to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the wheel on the motorcycle (Sect. G 1, Refitting the front wheel).
Refitting the front brake system
When reconnecting the brake hose to the calliper or master cylinder, make sure to install the sealing washers (2) on either side of the hose end fitting.
After orientating the coupling, tighten the screw (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Reconnect the hoses (3) and (15) to the front brake callipers (10) and (11), and tighten the screws (7) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
 Note
Make sure that internal hose coupling (3) and the external hose coupling (15) are installed in the right brake calliper (10).
Fit the left brake calliper (11) over the disc.
Apply the recommended grease to the screws (9).
Hand tighten the screws (9) with spacers (8) to secure the callipers to the fork legs.
Operate the brake lever (A) two or three times to pressurise the circuit so that the brake pads are brought into contact with the brake disc.
Repeat the operation for the other brake calliper.
Hold the lever pulled in towards the grip and tighten the calliper screws (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
 
Insert the front brake hose (3) inside the hose clip (20), and tighten the screw (23) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit front brake hose (3) in the hose clip (20) on mudguard, fixing it with screw (24).
Tighten screw (21) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Position the sealing washers (2) and tighten the screw (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Place the appropriate number of shims (27) under the speed sensor (26) so as to achieve a 2.0 to 2.3 mm air-gap between the speed sensor and the speed reading pin (P) (one among the five present on the front brake disc).
 Note
It is recommended to start with 3 shims.
 Note
Shimming shall be made with the speed sensor (26) locked inside its seat, it is thus necessary to loosen and tighten the screw (29) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) whenever adding or removing shims.
