Description of the clutch assembly
The clutch is disengaged by a transmission unit consisting of a thrust piston (C) accommodated inside a support mounted to the generator cover. This piston (C) pushes a pushrod (B), which runs through gearbox primary shaft and operates the pressure plate (4) located on top of the clutch plate pack (9).
Drive is transmitted from the crankshaft to the gearbox primary shaft by a gear integrated with the clutch housing/primary drive gear pair (A).
Accommodated in the clutch housing is a set of driving and driven plates (9). When the clutch is operated, the driven plates push away a drum (12) which is splined onto the gearbox primary shaft.
Before working on the internal clutch parts, check that the clutch operates correctly. Then deal with the problem in a systematic manner.
Following is a list of possible causes of clutch malfunctions.
 
A clutch which does not disengage may be caused by:
-
excessive play of the control lever;
-
distorted clutch plates;
-
incorrect spring tension;
-
faulty clutch release mechanism;
-
excessive wear of the hub or clutch housing.
 
A clutch which slips may be caused by:
-
no backlash of the control lever;
-
worn clutch plates;
-
weakened springs;
-
faulty clutch release mechanism;
-
excessive wear of the hub or clutch housing.
 
A noisy clutch may be caused by:
-
excessive backlash between the primary drive gears;
-
damaged primary drive gear teeth;
-
excessive housing between friction plate tabs and the clutch housing;
-
worn gear/clutch housing bearings;
-
the presence of metal particles (filings) on the gear teeth.

Undo the fixing screws (1) and remove the collars (2) and the springs (3) from the pressure plate (4).
Remove the pressure plate (4). Remove the clutch lifter (6). If necessary, using a drift of suitable diameter, remove the bearing (5).
Lock the clutch drum (12) with tool no. 88713.2651, insert the bush (D) and loosen the fastening nut (7).
Remove the nut (7) and the Belleville washer (8), slide the clutch drum (12) and remove the clutch plates (9) from the clutch housing.
Remove the clutch plates (9) from the clutch drum (12). When removing the clutch plates, keep them together in pairs in the correct order and set them aside, tying them together, if necessary.
Remove the spacer (13) from the primary shaft.
Withdraw the Belleville washer (10) and flat ring (11) from the clutch drum (12).
Checking and overhauling the components
Clearance between the clutch housing and friction plates
Insert a friction plate (E) in the clutch housing (F) and measure the clearance (S) with a feeler gauge.
Clearance “S” must not exceed 0.6 mm.
If it does, replace the plates and, if necessary, the clutch housing.
Overhauling the clutch plates
The clutch plates must not show any signs of blackening, grooves or deformation.
Measure the thickness of the friction plates; it should not be less than 2.6 mm.
Important
The total thickness of the plate pack must not be less than 46.1 mm.
 
Place the plate on a flat surface and check the amount of deformation with a feeler gauge.
Max. flatness error: 0.2 mm.
Overhauling the pressure plate
Check bearing (5) conditions: replace the bearing if the play is excessive.
Check the contact surfaces of the last friction plate; if extremely scored, polish it in the same manner as described previously for the cylinder head surface (Sect. N 4.5, Overhauling the cylinder head components).
Check the condition of the spring guide seats (G) of the pressure plate (4) and always replace the spring retainer collars (2) at each overhaul.
Overhauling the pressure plate springs
Measure the length “L” of each spring (3).
Min. length: 41 mm.
Replace any springs that are shorter than the above limit value.
Reassembling the clutch
Fit the spacer (13) on the primary shaft: the spacer (13) shall be centred on the gearbox primary shaft axis.
Fit the flat ring (11) and the Belleville washer (10) on the clutch drum (12), so that the convex side faces the clutch drum.
Locate the Belleville washer (8).
Apply the prescribed grease to the thread of the gearbox primary shaft and the mating surface of nut (7), and fit it over Belleville washer (8).
Lock the clutch drum with tool no. 88713.2651, insert bush (D) inside tool, and tighten the retaining nut (7) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Install the clutch plates (9) in the following order:
-
one driving plate (H) with 48 square notches;
-
a series of three driven plates (I) thickness 1.6 mm alternately to three friction plates (L) with 40 rectangular notches;
-
a series of four driven plates (M) thickness 2 mm alternately to four friction plates (L) with 40 rectangular notches;
-
a series of two driven plates (I) thickness 1.6 mm alternately to two friction plates (L) with 40 rectangular notches;
-
one plate (I) 1.6 mm thick;
-
one friction plate (N) with 48 rectangular notches.
 Note
The last clutch plate shall be positioned with a different direction compared to the previous ones, as shown in the figure.
 
Lubricate primary shaft inner hole with engine oil.
Insert the control pin (6) in the bearing (5) and the latter in the gearbox primary shaft.
Fit the pressure plate (4).
In each cavity, fit a spring (3) and a collar (2).
Lubricate the thread of the screws (1) with oil.
Tighten the screws (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values), in a crossed-pattern sequence.
