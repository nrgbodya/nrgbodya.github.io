Protection and safety devices
Checking protection and safety device components
Checking the ignition switch
Disconnect the ignition key (A) from the electric system by opening its connection (Sect. P 1, Table I) and use a multimeter to check continuity of its internal connections as follows:
-
turn the key to off () and connect a multimeter to contacts (1) and (4) to check electrical continuity (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments, related to the multimeter operation). The resistance reading should be near zero and, if present, the audible continuity signal should be emitted;
-
turn the key to on () and connect a multimeter to contacts (1) and (2) and then to (3) and (4) to check for electrical continuity. The resistance reading should be near zero and, if present, the audible continuity signal should be emitted;
-
turn the key to park () and connect the multimeter to contacts (1) and (4) to check electrical continuity. The resistance reading should be near zero and, if present, the audible continuity signal should be emitted;
-
turn the key to lock () and connect the multimeter to contacts (1) and (4) to check the electrical continuity. The resistance reading should be near zero and, if present, the continuity sound signal should be emitted.
 Note
The same test may be done using the “DDS 2” tester (Sect. D 5, DDS 2 (Ducati Diagnosis System 2.0) diagnosis instrument.).
Checking the side stand switch
Remove the switch (1) from the side stand and disconnect the main wiring connector from the switch (Sect. P 1, Table T).
Use an analogue or digital multimeter (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments) to check switch operation (see table).

Checking the fuses
The main fuse box (1) is located under the rider seat.
Lift rider seat edges, close to brackets, and loosen the retaining screws (2).
To remove rider seat from vehicle, slightly pull it back, and lift it.
The fuses are accessed by removing the cover, which shows the ampere rating and mounting locations.
For ampere ratings, refer to the chapter “Wiring diagram”, Sect. P 1.
Important
Before replacing a damaged fuse with a new one of the same rating, identify the cause of the problem.
 
A blown fuse can be identified by the breakage of inner filament (A).
Important
To avoid possible short circuits, replace the fuse with key turned to OFF.
Warning
Never use a fuse with a rating other than the specified value. Failure to observe this rule may damage the electric system or even cause fire.
 
As well as the fuses in the fuse box, the vehicle has a 30 A fuse (B), located on rear subframe RH side, that protects the electronic regulator (Sect. P 2, Rectifier-regulator).
