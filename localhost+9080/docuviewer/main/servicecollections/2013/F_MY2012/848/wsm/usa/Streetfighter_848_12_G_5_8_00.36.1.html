Note
The rear hub is a component critical to the dynamic safety of the motorcycle. For this reason, any operation on the internal components of the hub is strictly forbidden. The hub is available as complete assembly, part no. 756.2.007.1A.
Refitting the rear wheel eccentric hub and rear wheel shaft
Refitting is the reverse of removal, with attention to the following points.
If the calliper bracket locating pin (2) was removed, apply the recommended threadlocker upon reassembly.
Before refitting, apply recommended grease on the eccentric hub (17) seat of the calliper mounting bracket (16) where the previously greased rings (15) will be installed. Position the bracket (16), making sure that the indicated point matches with the anti-rotation pin (2).
Fit the first washer (14) with the sharp edge facing the eccentric hub (17).
 
Refit the other washer (14), with the sharp edge always facing the eccentric hub.
Apply an even coating of the recommended grease in the areas (A) of the shaft (27).
Insert the rear wheel shaft (27) in the hub.
Ensure the wheel shaft (27) is seated in the hub.
Fit the inner ring (24) on the wheel shaft (27).
Insert the inner ring (24) in the hub.
Apply recommended grease to screws (3) and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) in the sequence 1-2-1.
Turn the eccentric hub (17) taking the horizontal axis (A) in the hub hole (B) below the horizontal axis (C) of the hub seat .

Before refitting the swinging arm shaft (1), check it carefully for distortion.

Overhauling the rear swinging arm
Inside the swinging arm (4), in correspondence with the pivot point on the frame, there is a pair of ball bearings (19) and a spacer (20) on the RH side, and a pair of needle roller bearings (25), with sealing rings (23), on the LH side.
To change the bearings, proceed as follows.
Remove the spacers (12) and (21) fitted on the RH side of the swinging arm, and the bush (22) on the LH side.
Remove the ball bearings (19), the sealing rings (23) and the roller bearings (25) with a suitable punch and press. Support the swinging arm and take care not to damage the bearing bores upon removal.
Important
Once removed, the bearings (19), the sealing rings (23) and the roller bearings (25) must not be reinstalled.
Heat the entire swinging arm up to 150 °C while supporting its weight adequately.
Insert the new needle roller bearings (25) in the drift tool no. 88713.1068, and install them into the bore on the left-hand side of the swinging arm, working from the outside.
Drive them inside until the tool is fully seated against the swinging arm.
Use the same tool to fit the new sealing rings (23), positioned as shown in the sectional view, so that they seat against the newly installed roller bearings.
To install the ball bearings (19) you will need the tool no. 88713.2409 with:
(A) – drift for internal bearing;
(A) – drift for external bearing;
(C) – guide pin.
 
Fit a new bearing (19) and the internal spacer (20) onto the drift (A) and install it on the inner side of the swinging arm RH mounting.
Insert the guide pin (C) into the previously mounted needle roller bearings and insert the other end in the bore in the drift (A).
Drive the bearing (19) fully into the swinging arm.
Fit the other new bearing (19) at the outer end of drift (A).
Using drift (A) as a stop, use drift (B) to drive the external bearing up against the spacer (20): remove the tools.
Before refitting the swinging arm (4), grease the ball bearings (19), the needle roller bearings (25) and the sealing rings (23) with the recommended grease.
Refit the spacers (21) and (12) on the right-hand side of the swinging arm (4) and refit the bush (22) on the left-hand side.
Refitting the rear swinging arm
Insert the upper chain slider (7) in the swinging arm opening, and secure it in place using the screws (9) and (10) and the washer (11).
Refit the rear brake hose and speed sensor onto the swinging arm (4), positioning the clip (D); secure to the lower chain slider (8) with the screws (9).
Apply recommended threadlocker on the screws (9) and (10), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Locate the swinging arm (4) on the frame.
Lubricate the swinging arm shaft (1) with recommended grease and insert it fully into the frame.
Working on bike opposite side, apply a layer of recommended grease, and secure the swinging arm shaft (1) with nut (5).
Tighten the nut (5) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Connect the speed sensor to the main wiring (Sect. P 1, Routing of wiring on frame).
