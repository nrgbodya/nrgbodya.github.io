Cylinder head: timing system
Important
Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
 
Note
For clarity, the figures show the engine removed from the frame.
 
To work with the engine installed on the frame, first proceed as follows:

 Note
Remove also any parts which may impede the procedure.
Removing the timing belt covers
Undo the fixing screws (4) of the external cover (1) and remove it from the vertical piston-cylinder assy.
Undo the fixing screws (4) of the external cover (3) and remove it from the horizontal piston-cylinder assy.
Removing the tensioner pulley/timing belt
Loosen the nut (8) and remove the washer (7) and the tensioner pulley (9) from the pin (12) on the cylinder head.
Remove the timing belt (14) from the piston-cylinder assy.
Important
If the belts are to be re-used, mark the direction of rotation with an arrow and also mark the piston-cylinder assy they belong to.
 
Repeat the procedure for the other piston-cylinder assy.
Disassembling the tensioner pulley
Remove the circlip (27).
Remove the eccentric screw (28) from the bush (25).
Remove the circlip (26) and slide the bearing (30) out of the bush (25).
Reassembling the tensioner pulley
Reassemble the components in the reverse order of disassembling.
Removing the cylinder head pulley/idler pulley
Insert the tool no. 88713.1806 in the pulleys to lock their rotation and use the supplied bush to loosen the fixing ring nuts (21) of the pulleys.
Important
Upon reassembly, always use new ring nuts.
 
Remove the ring nuts (21) and the pulleys (11) from the camshafts.
Undo the screw (5) and remove the idler pulley (13) from the pin (6).
Repeat the same procedure to remove the other tensioner.
Removing the tensioner pins
Using the tool no. 88713.1821, remove the tensioner pins (12) and (6) from the cylinder heads. Recover the spacer (2) of the pin (6).
Important
Upon reassembly, take care not to invert the position of pins (12) and (6).
Disassembling the camshaft pulleys
Undo and remove the screws (22).
Slide off the washer (23).
Withdraw the camshaft pulley (11) from the spacer flange (24).
Removing the timing layshaft pulleys
Use the tool no. 88713.1805 to hold the driving pulleys on the engine crankcase against rotation.
Important
If this operation is carried out with the engine installed in the frame, hold the driving pulleys against rotation using the tool no. 88713.2011 mounted on the generator cover.
 
Loosen the ring nut (15) using the socket supplied with the tool.
Remove the ring nut (15), the spacer (20) and the outer pulley (18).
Remove the first key (16) from the timing layshaft.
Remove the spacer (19) and the inner pulley (18).
Remove the inner spacer (17) and second key (16) on the timing layshaft.
It is now possible to remove the snap ring (10) on the timing layshaft.
Refitting the timing layshaft pulley
To fit the snap ring (10) in the timing layshaft seat, use the tool no. 88713.2834.
Install the inner spacer (17) on the timing layshaft, taking care to align the notch in the spacer with the slot in the key.
Fit the first key (16) on the timing layshaft.
Locate the inner pulley (18).
Refit the second key (16) and the washer (19).
Locate the outer pulley (18) and the spacer (20).
Apply the recommended grease to the threads of the shaft.
Fit the ring nut (15).
Important
To prevent the ring nuts working loose and consequent serious engine damage, always use new self-locking ring nuts on all the timing belt pulleys on reassembly.
 
Lock rotation of the pulleys by means of the tool no. 88713.1805 and, using the insert supplied with the wrench together with a torque wrench, tighten the self-locking nut to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Refitting the idler and tensioner pulley mounting pins
Apply the recommended threadlocker to the pin thread.
Insert the tensioner pins (12) and (6) with the spacers (2) onto the heads, and screw them using tool no. 88713.1821.
Tighten the tensioner pins to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Reassembling the timing pulleys
Fit the pulley (11) on the flange (24), aligning the timing pulley mark (D) on the pulley with the flange timing mark (E).
Install the washer (23) up against the pulley, aligning the timing notch (F) with the pulley and flange timing marks.
Insert the three screws (22) in the threaded holes (G) of the flange.
Tighten the screws (22) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Refitting the cylinder heads pulleys/idler pulleys
Check that the keyway on the end of the camshafts is in good condition and without burrs.
Fit a key (B) in the keyway of each camshaft.
Fit the pulley (11) on the camshaft, inserting the key in the in the pulley slot (C).
Apply the recommended grease to the threads of the camshaft.
Repeat the procedure on the other camshaft.
Insert the tool no. 88713.1806 into the pulleys to prevent their rotation.
Apply the recommended grease to the mating face of the ring nut (21).
Fit the ring nut (21).
Carry out the same operations on the other camshaft.
Important
Always fit new nuts on reassembly.
Using the bush supplied with tool no. 88713.1806, and a torque wrench, tighten the ring nuts (21) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Insert the idler pulleys (13), with bearings, inside pins (6) on heads, and grease the screws (5), then tighten them to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Undo the locking screws (22) of the pulleys, by turning them counter clockwise by 90 5.
Check that the pulleys have no end float and can rotate freely at all points along the full length of the slots.
Refitting the timing belts
Rotate the pulleys on the timing layshaft until the timing mark on the outer pulley is aligned with the mark on the clutch cover.
In this condition, the horizontal cylinder piston will be at top dead centre.
Install in the generator cover seat the tool no. 88713.2011 to prevent crankshaft rotation and lock it with the corresponding pin, by tightening the M8 screw of the tool to 20 Nm.
Keep the camshafts blocked with tools no. 88765.1524: one on vertical head and the other one on horizontal head.
Lock tools on camshafts with pin (C).
Important
To achieve correct timing, the screws (22) securing the pulleys to the hubs must be loose and positioned in the centre of their slots.
Fit the vertical piston-cylinder assy timing belt around the camshaft pulleys and pass it behind the idler pulley.
Repeat the procedure on the horizontal belt.
 Note
If the used belts are to be refitted, position them in their original direction of rotation and on their original cylinder.
 
Fit the tensioner pulley (9) and the washer (7) on the pin (12) of each cylinder head.
Apply recommended grease on tensioner pulley threads, and on nuts (8) mating surfaces.
Start the nut (8).
Check the timing belt tension and adjust if necessary as described in the paragraph "Checking and adjusting timing belt tension" (Sect. D 5).
Warning
Check the tension on the belt sections (D) and (E) shown in the figure.
When the correct belt tensioning value is reached, ensure that tensioner pulley (9) retaining nuts (8) and screws (22) are tightened to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Remove the camshaft locking tool no. 88765.1524 and the crankshaft locking tool no. 88713.2011.
Refitting the timing outer covers
If removed, fit the airbox (A) with cover (B), and tighten screws (C).
Fit the horizontal cylinder outer cover (3) starting the screws (4).
Repeat the same procedure for the vertical cylinder outer cover (1).
Tighten the screws (4) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values), observing the sequence indicated in the figure.
 Note
If the above operations have been carried out with the engine installed in the frame, refit the previously removed parts.
