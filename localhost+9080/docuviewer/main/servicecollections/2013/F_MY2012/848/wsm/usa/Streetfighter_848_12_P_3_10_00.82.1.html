Electric starting system
The key components of the electric starting system are a solenoid starter (6) and a starter motor (5) fed by the battery (7).
The engine starting strategy is managed entirely by the engine ECU (34), which monitors the following inputs:
-
Engine Stop switch on right-hand switch (1)
-
Engine Start switch on right-hand switch (1)
-
Neutral sensor (31)
-
Side stand sensor (29)
-
Clutch microswitch (36).
 
When these inputs are combined in the required manner, the engine control unit (34) enables engine starting.

Starter motor
Power:
-
0.7 kW/12 V.
Direction of rotation:
-
counter clockwise viewed from power takeoff side.
The starter motor is highly compact and reliable and therefore rarely gives any type of problem. In case of malfunction, ensure that the starter motor wiring terminal is properly tightened under the nut and shows no sign of oxidation. If the terminal is properly tightened and free from oxidation, remove the starter motor and test it under no-load conditions (no load applied to the shaft). Secure the starter motor to a test bench, making sure not to damage the casing. Use a fully charged 12 V battery for the test. Use battery-motor connection cables which are no longer than 70 cm and with the same cross-section of the cable on the motorcycle itself. Connect the negative terminal of the battery to an unpainted area of the starter motor casing and the positive terminal to its electrical terminal. The shaft of the starter motor should rotate freely and at high speed. Take care not to short-circuit the two cables connected to the battery.

Note
The starter motor retaining screws are assembled with threadlocker.
 
Slide out the starter motor and gasket (5).
Refitting the starter motor
Visually inspect the condition of the gasket (5) and replace if necessary.
Position the gasket (5) and the starter motor on the crankcase. Start the retaining screws (3), the lower one (3) with the ground cable (6), the toothed washer (7) and the washer (8). Tighten the screws (3) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Locate the insert (2) (if removed) in the crankcase half, and tighten the fixing screw (1) to the specified torque (Sect. 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Connect the starter motor/solenoid starter cable (4).
Important
Fill the cap with protective grease before fitting it on the starter motor.

Loosen the screw (7), and remove the solenoid starter (1) from the battery support.
Checking operation of the solenoid starter
Apply 12 V (battery voltage) across the positive terminal (A) and negative terminal (B) of the connector.
With a multimeter connected between the two poles (threaded pins (C) and (D)) of the solenoid starter, check for electric continuity (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments). If there is no electrical continuity, replace the solenoid starter.
 
Ensure that the terminals are not oxidised and apply water repellent spray.
