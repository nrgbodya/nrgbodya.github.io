Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Canister filter system (USA versions only)
USA models are equipped with an additional system with a Canister filter that prevents fuel fumes from being discharged into the atmosphere.
The breather hose (4) is connected to the Canister filter (1); when the fuel has been filtered, it is returned through the hose (6) to the horizontal (A) and vertical (B) intake manifolds via the hoses (9) and (10). Breather hose (15) and hose (2) vent to the ground.
For correct positioning of the connection hoses consult the indications given below.
 
Removing the canister filter
Before removing the Canister filter (1), lift tank (Sect. L 2, Removing the fuel tank).
Disconnect the hose (4), removing the clamp (11).
Remove the hose clips (7) and (5), and remove the Canister filter (1) sliding it from top on plate (17) guides.
 
To remove the hoses feeding fuel to the intake manifolds, remove the clips (11), and slide out the hoses (9) and (10).
Refitting the Canister filter
To refit, carry out the removal operations in reverse order, making sure to locate the hoses as shown in the figures at the end of the chapter.
