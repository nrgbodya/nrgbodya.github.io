Removing the exhaust system
Operations
Section reference
Remove the cowling
E 2, Removing the cowling
Remove the springs (13) from the silencers (28).
Undo the screw (29) and collect the nut (31).
Working on both sides, loosen the screws (45).
Loosen the screw (35) and recover the nut (31), the two rubber pads (33) and the spacer (34).
Support the exhaust silencers (28), and keep the two clamps (30), the spacer (44) and the two sealing washers (32).
Remove the exhaust silencers (28).
Loosen the screws (8), and keep the spacers (7) and the washers (6).
Remove the heat guard (36).
Undo the two retaining screws (27) and keep the guard (26).
Loosen the screw (21) and the nut (25) securing the cable (20) to the exhaust valve.
Keep the spacer (22) and the Belleville washers (23).
Remove the cable (20) from the exhaust valve pulley (A).
Loosen the screw (47), keeping the spacer (43) and the quick-release fastener (46), then release the cable grommet (41) with the rubber pad (42).
Loosen the four retaining screws (C) of the exhaust valve actuator protection (D).
Loosen the nuts (P) on the screws (E) to remove the exhaust valve motor (48) from frame, then disconnect the exhaust valve motor (48) wiring (F).
Disconnect the vertical cylinder Lambda sensor (12) from wiring (H).
Disconnect the horizontal cylinder Lambda sensor (12) from wiring (G).
Loosen the screw (17) and nut (15) securing pipe (14) to frame, and keep the washers (16).
Remove the springs (13) and slide the vertical cylinder head exhaust pipe (14) from the central exhaust pipe (24) and from the exhaust manifold (37).
Remove the spring (13) and slide the central exhaust pipe (24) out of the horizontal cylinder head exhaust pipe (11).
Loosen the nuts (1) and remove the heat guard (2) from the exhaust manifold (37).
Unscrew the nuts (3) and remove the exhaust manifold (37) from the vertical cylinder head and the exhaust pipe (11) from the horizontal cylinder head.
Refitting the exhaust system
Position the exhaust manifold (37) on the vertical cylinder head and the horizontal cylinder head exhaust pipe (11) on the horizontal cylinder head with gaskets (4).
Tighten the nuts (3) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the heat guard (2) on the exhaust manifold (37), by tightening the nuts (1).
Connect the central exhaust pipe (24) to the horizontal cylinder head pipe (11) with the spring (13).
Secure the vertical cylinder head exhaust pipe (14) to the exhaust manifold (37) and to the central exhaust pipe (24) with the springs (13).
Secure the vertical cylinder head exhaust pipe (14) to the frame with the screw (17) and the nut (15), then tighten the screw (17) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Connect the horizontal cylinder Lambda sensor (12) to wiring (G).
Connect the vertical cylinder Lambda sensor (12) to wiring (H).
Check that the cable cylindrical terminal (T) is fully seated inside exhaust valve motor.
Fit the exhaust valve motor (48) on the frame, by tightening the nuts (P) on the screws (E) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Connect the motor to the wiring (F).
Position the exhaust valve actuator protection (D), and tighten the four retaining screws (C) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Insert the quick-release fastener (46) on the bracket (B).
Position the cable grommet (41) with rubber pad (42), as shown in the figure, then start the retaining screw (47) with spacer (43).
Tighten the screw (47) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Insert exhaust valve cable (20) end fitting (L) inside its seat on the pulley (A), as shown in the figure.
After assembly, the cable (20) on exhaust valve and the relevant motor is adjusted automatically by the instrument panel software, that causes the valve to open fully at the first key-on, and then to go back to rest position with the valve fully closed.
 
Start the screw (21) with the spacer (22) and fit the Belleville washer (23) inside the exhaust valve cable (20) bracket (M), then insert the other Belleville washer (23) on the opposite side, as shown in the figure.
Position the bracket (M) onto the central exhaust pipe (24) bracket (N), aiming it as shown in the figure; then start the nut (25).
Tighten the screw (21) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) while holding nut (25).
Fit the guard (26), and tighten the two retaining screws (27) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Make sure that the quick-release fasteners (5) are present on the exhaust brackets, and aim them as shown in the figure.
Insert the three spacers (7) with washers (6) on the screws (8).
Insert the three screws (8) in the holes of the heat guard (36).
Start the screws (8), and fit the heat guard (36). Place the three spacers (7) and the three washers (6) between the heat guard and the exhaust pipe.
 Note
The spacers (7) shall be aimed as shown in the figure.
Tighten the screws (8) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Position the two silencers (28) on the central exhaust pipe (24).
Support the two silencers, and position the two sealing washers (32), the spacer (44) and the two clamps (30) as shown in the figure.
Fit the two rubber pads (33), the spacer (34) and the nut (31), and tighten the screw (35) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Working on both sides, tighten the screws (45), to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit the nut (31) and tighten the screw (29) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit the springs (13) on the silencers (28).
If removed, insert the copper washers (10) inside the plugs (9), start the plugs inside the vertical and horizontal cylinder head exhaust manifolds, and tighten the plugs to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
