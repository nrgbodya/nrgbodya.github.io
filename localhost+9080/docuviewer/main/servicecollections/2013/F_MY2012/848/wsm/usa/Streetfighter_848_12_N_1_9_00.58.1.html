Removing the engine
In order to remove engine you must first remove a series of other components from the motorcycle.
Most of these removal procedures are described in the relative sections of this manual.
The following flow chart illustrates the logical sequence in which the parts are to be removed from the motorcycle and a reference to the section where the removal procedure is described.
This section describes only the operations to be carried out after having removed all the parts listed in the flow chart.

Place a support beneath the engine to support it during removal from the frame.
Loosen the nuts (2) on the right side of the frame, in correspondence to the two engine upper supporting screws (1).
Keep the swinging arm shaft (4) on the bike LH side, and loosen at the same time the screw (3) with washer (5) on the opposite side:
Using the drift no. 88713.1074, fully extract the swinging arm shaft (4).
In this way the swinging arm is not fixed to the engine any more, and can be left on the frame.
Remove the two upper screws (1).
Withdraw the complete engine assembly from the frame by lowering it and pushing it forwards.
Refitting the engine
Refitting is the reverse of removal.
Important
Apply recommended grease and tighten the nuts (2) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the removed parts by performing the steps shown in the table and in the specific sections of the manual in reverse order.
