5 -
Indicating devices
Checking the indicating devices
In the event of a fault, the internal connections of the device must be checked in all operating conditions. To do this, it is necessary to disconnect the switch connector from the main wiring (Sect. P 1, Routing of wiring on frame).
Then analyse the switch using an analogue or digital multimeter (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments).
Note
The same test may be done using the “DDS 2” tester (Sect. D 5, DDS 2 (Ducati Diagnosis System 2.0) diagnosis instrument.).
Checking the left-hand switch
To remove the left-hand switch, undo the fixing screws (1) and disconnect it from the electric system.
The colours mentioned in the following descriptions refer to the colours of the wires from the switch and not to the colours of the main electric system wires.
 
HORN button
Connect the terminals of a multimeter to the Blue/White and Brown cables to check for electric continuity, which must be available when HORN is pressed (see Sect. P 9, "Diagnosis instruments", related to multimeter operation). When the horn button is pressed, the resistance value shown by the multimeter should be close to zero and, if available, a continuity beep should be heard. When the horn button is not pressed, the resistance value should be infinity (there is no continuity as the electrical contacts inside the push-button are open) and no continuity beep should be heard. If these conditions are not met, the device must be replaced.
Turn indicator switch (TURN)
Connect the multimeter to the Orange and Grey wires arriving from the turn indicator switch and check for electrical continuity when operating the right turn signal (see Sect. P 9, "Diagnosis instruments", related to the multimeter operation). Repeat the above procedure for the left turn indicator, but connect the multimeter to the Green and Orange wires. Colours mentioned in the descriptions refer to the colour of wires from the switch and not to the colour of wires of the main electric system.
 
Low and high beams (DIMMER)
Test using the same procedure, applying the probes of the meter to the Red/Blue and Light blue/Yellow wires.
 
Control switch (MODE)
Connect the multimeter to the Brown and Blue/Yellow wires arriving from the instrument panel function selector switch and check that when operating the push-button (2) in the position (s) there is electric continuity (Sect. P 9, "Diagnosis instruments", related to the multimeter operation). Repeat the same procedure, operating the push button (3) in the position (t), by connecting the multimeter to the Blue/Yellow and Orange wires.
Flasher (PASSING)
Check for continuity between the Red/Blue and Brown wires.
Refit the left-hand switch by tightening the screws (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Checking the right-hand switch
To remove the right-hand switch, undo the retaining screws (1) and disconnect the wiring connector from the electric system.
The colours mentioned in the following descriptions refer to the colours of the wires from the switch and not to the colours of the main electric system wires.
 
Engine Stop button
Using a multimeter, check for continuity between the Red/Black and Red/White wires (see Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments related to the multimeter operation). When the button is in RUN position, there should be electrical continuity between the two wires. When the button is in the OFF position, there should be no electrical continuity between the two wires. If these conditions are not met, the engine stop switch is not working correctly and must be replaced.
 
STARTER button
Proceed as described for the engine stop button and check for continuity between the Blue/White and Black wires when the starter button is pressed (see Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments related to the multimeter operation). If there is no continuity, the starter button is faulty and must be replaced. Colours mentioned in the descriptions refer to the colour of wires from the switch and not to the colour of the main electric system wires.
Refit the right-hand switch by tightening the screws (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Checking the front and rear brake light, neutral light, oil pressure and clutch switches
Brake light switches
To check operation of the front (1) and rear (2) brake light switches use a multimeter: when the front or rear brake is pressed, there must be electric continuity (Pos. A) between the terminals of the corresponding switch (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments, related to operation of the multimeter). No electric continuity should be available when brakes are not enabled (Pos.B). If these tests fail to produce positive results, the part in question must be replaced.
Neutral light
To check the neutral light switch (3) proceed as follows.
The neutral light does not illuminate on the dashboard.
Remove the electric terminal connected to the neutral switch. Switch on the dashboard (ignition key turned to ON) and ensure that the light illuminates when the terminal is earthed. If the light switches on, the neutral light switch should be changed. If the light stays off, switch off the dashboard (ignition key turned to OFF) and check for electrical continuity between the neutral switch and engine ECU with a multimeter.
The neutral light on the dashboard is steady on.
Switch on the dashboard (ignition key turned to ON) and remove the electrical terminal from the neutral switch. If the light turns off, the neutral switch should be changed. If the light stays on, switch off the dashboard (ignition key turned to OFF) and use a multimeter to check whether the section of circuit between neutral switch and engine ECU is earthed.
 
Oil pressure sensor
To test the operation of the engine oil pressure sensor (4), proceed as follows.
Use the diagnosis tester to check that oil pressure into the engine lubrication circuit complies with the specified values (Sect. D 5, Checking the engine oil pressure).
If the engine oil pressure value is outside the specified range, check the lubrication circuit components and service as necessary.
If engine oil pressure value is within the specified range and the oil pressure warning light on the dashboard stays off, switch on the instrument panel (ignition key turned to ON) without starting the engine, and disconnect the electrical terminal from the pressure sensor and connect it to earth. If the warning light now illuminates, this means the sensor is defective and must be replaced. If the warning light does not illuminate, use a multimeter and check for electrical continuity in the section of the circuit between sensor and warning light on the instrument panel (this check must be performed with ignition key turned to OFF, i.e. with dashboard off).
If the engine oil pressure complies with the stated values and the engine oil pressure warning light on the instrument panel is continuously illuminated, switch on the dashboard (ignition key turned to ON) and start the engine, then disconnect the electrical terminal normally inserted on the pressure sensor. If the warning light now switches off, this means the sensor is defective. If the warning light does not switch off, use a multimeter to check that the section of the circuit between sensor and warning light on the instrument panel is not connected to earth (this check must be performed with the ignition key turned to OFF, i.e. dashboard off).
Clutch switch
For the clutch switch (5) proceed in the same manner as for the brake light switches (see beginning of this paragraph).
Water temperature sensor
The coolant temperature sensor (6) sends the electric signal to the engine ECU, sharing it with the instrument panel through the CAN line. Check sensor (6) operation with the "DDS 2" Diagnosis Tester connected to ECU diagnosis socket. After having made the selections indicated on tester menu, read the engine coolant temperature value among the different displayed engine parameters. With cold engine, the "DDS 2" air temperature reading shall be similar to that of the coolant. With hot engine and cooling fan just enabled, coolant temperature shall be similar to that of the electric fan. Should the outcomes of these tests be negative, replace the engine coolant temperature sensor. Should the "DDS 2" tester detect an error such as "coolant temperature sensor short circuit or interrupted circuit" inside the ECU memory, check the electric system connecting sensor to ECU. If this section of the electric system is not faulty (no interruptions, no short circuits, no earthing), replace the sensor.
Fan relay
The fan relay (7) is positioned onto the battery support.
To gain access to this component, remove the rider seat (Sect. E 3, Rider Seat Removal).
Checking the electric fan relay operation
Disconnect the relay from the electric system, and apply a voltage of 12 V (battery) across the small contacts: you should hear a click that confirms that the internal electromagnet has switched.
Connect a multimeter to the big contacts to check for electrical continuity (Sect. P 9, Diagnosis instruments). The resistance reading should be near zero and, if present, the continuity sound signal should be emitted. If this does not occur, the part must be replaced.
Changing light bulbs
Changing the turn indicator light bulbs
Undo the screw (1) and detach the lens (2) from the turn indicator support (3).
The bulb has a bayonet joint: to remove it, push it in and turn it counter-clockwise.
To remove the support (3), for the front turn indicators refer to Sect. H 8, Removing the headlight, while for the rear turn indicators refer to Sect. H 7, Removing the number plate holder.
Fit the spare bulb by pressing and turning clockwise until it clicks into its seat.
Refit the lens by inserting the tab (A) in the corresponding slot in the turn indicator support.
Tighten the screw (1).
