Removing the clutch cover

Undo the screw (7) placed at the bottom end of the clutch cover.
Loosen the fastening screws (2), (3), (5) and (18) on the clutch cover (1).
Tap around the edge of the cover with a plastic mallet to detach it from the crankcase half.
Remove the clutch cover (1) paying attention to the centring bush (12).
Check the condition of the centring bush (12) and replace if deformed.
Disassembling the clutch cover
Remove the plug (14) from the cover and the relative O-ring (13), the engine oil pressure switch (17) and its sealing washer (16).
Undo the fixing screw (20) of the inner cover (19).
Remove the inner cover (19) and soundproofing panel (21).
Remove the circlip (10) and withdraw the shim (9) and the sealing ring (8).
The drilled bush (7) is mounted to the cover by a forced interference fit. Remove it using a suitable puller.
Visually inspect the sealing ring (8) and replace it if necessary.
Reassembling the clutch cover
Fit the plug (14) and the gasket (13). Install the pressure switch (17) with its sealing washer (16).
If the bush has been replaced, fully seat the new bush (7) in the slot in the cover using a suitable drift and a press.
If the sealing ring (8) needs to be replaced, fit the new seal into the crankcase cover, positioning it so that the side without a spring is facing the circlip (10).
Before fitting, check that the edges of the circlip groove show no sign of burrs which might otherwise damage the sealing ring.
Then fit the shim (9) and the circlip (10).
Important
In order for the sealing ring (8) to function correctly, it must have an assembly clearance that allows the circlip (10) to rotate.
Position the soundproofing panel (21) inside the clutch cover (1), centring it with the pin (D) as shown in the figure.
Position the inner cover (19) on the panel (21), centring it with the pin (D).
Fit and tighten the screw (20) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Refitting the clutch cover
Clean and degrease mating surfaces on cover and crankcase half, and ensure that centring bush (12) and the O-ring (11), located in correspondence with the oil way, are installed on the crankcase.
Apply an even, regular bead of DUCATI sealing compound (A) on the mating surface of the crankcase half and around all holes.
Fit the cover assembly (1) to the crankcase half and insert the retaining screws. Tighten the screws (2), (3), (5) and (18) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values) in a crossed-pattern sequence.
