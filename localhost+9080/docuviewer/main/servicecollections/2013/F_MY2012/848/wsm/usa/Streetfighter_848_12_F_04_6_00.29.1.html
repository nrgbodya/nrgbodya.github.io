Rear brake
Important
Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Removing of the rear brake control
Warning
The brake master cylinder manufacturer advises against servicing the brake master cylinder due to the safety critical nature of this component.
Incorrect overhaul can endanger the rider and passenger safety.
 
Maintenance operations on these units are limited to renewal of the following parts: control lever, fluid reservoir assembly and relative fasteners and master cylinder fasteners.
 
Operations
Section reference
Drain the braking system
D 4, Changing the brake fluid
Disconnect the rear stop switch (5) from main wiring (Sect. P 1, Routing of wiring on frame).
Release hoses (7) and (21) from clips (A).
Undo the special screw (22) from master cylinder (16), and slide out hose (7), recovering the sealing washers (23).
Loosen rear brake master cylinder retaining screws (19).
Slide the adjusting rod (18) out of the rear brake master cylinder (16).
Undo screw (24), and remove fluid reservoir (25) with hose (21) from frame.
Undo the screws (2) securing master cylinder holder bracket (1) to engine.
Remove rear brake control from vehicle.
Loosen pivot screw (9) securing pedal (6) to master cylinder holder bracket (1).
Remove pedal (6) and recover washer (3) and O-rings (11).
Release spring (4), and remove it from master cylinder holder bracket (1).
Disassembling the rear brake control
The brake master cylinder is supplied only as a complete unit; internal components cannot be replaced.
To disassemble the master cylinder's outer parts, follow the indications given in the exploded view at the beginning of this Section.
If the bush (10) inside the brake pedal (6) needs to be replaced, grease the external surface and fit the new bush using a press to insert it. The bush must be placed at 2 mm from the pedal external face.
To disassemble the various parts of the system, refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this chapter.
Warning
After performing an operation on the rear brake control, check the brake pedal position following the instructions detailed in Sect. D 4, Adjusting the position of the gear change and rear brake pedals.
Refitting the rear brake control
Apply grease on the inner surface of the bush (10) and threadlocker on the pivot screw (9).
Install O-ring (11) onto pivot screw (9).
Insert pivot screw (9) on brake control lever (6), fit the other O-ring (11) and washer (3).
Fit brake control lever (6) on holder bracket (1) and tighten pivot screw (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit rear brake return spring (4) with end (A) onto special screw (8) and end (B) on plate (1), as shown in the figure.
Position master cylinder holder bracket (1) on the vehicle starting the retaining screws (2).
Tighten the screws (2) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Apply the recommended threadlocker on the screws (19).
Position the rear brake master cylinder (16) on the vehicle inserting the fixing screws (19).
Insert the rod (18) inside the master cylinder (16), after applying the recommended grease.
Position the reservoir (25) on its plate on the frame, and secure it with the screw (24), tightened to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Reconnect the hose (7), locating the sealing washers (23) on both sides of the hose end union, and secure it with the special screw (22).
Connect rear stop switch (5) to main wiring.
Secure hoses (7) and (21) in place with clips (A).
Tighten the special screw (22) and screws (19) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Note
If the hose (7) connecting the master cylinder to the brake calliper is changed, ensure that the hose end unions are positioned correctly on master cylinder.
