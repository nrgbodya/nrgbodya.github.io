Important
Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Removing the front mudguard
Undo the screw (7) and remove the front brake lines (A) from the hose grommet (4).
Undo and remove the retaining screws (5), and recover nylon washers (6).
Remove the front mudguard (1).
Note
On USA versions, a reflector is mounted on the front mudguard (1).
Note
The hose grommet (4) is fastened to front mudguard (1) by means of a shear rivet (3) with two washers (2) in-between.
Refitting the front mudguard
Warning
Do not use the motorcycle without the front mudguard fitted to avoid the risk of the brake hoses fouling the wheel on braking.
 
Position the front mudguard (1) and start the retaining screws (5) with relevant spacers (6), aiming them as shown in the figure.
Position the rubber block (B) present on front brake hose inside mudguard hose clip (4).
Fold hose clip (4) and fasten it by starting screw (7) into its thread.
Tighten the screws (5) and (7) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Removing the rear mudguard
Loosen the screws (10), recover spacers (9) and remove the rear mudguard (8) from swinging arm.
Refitting the rear mudguard
Position the rear mudguard (8) on the swinging arm starting screws (7) with spacers (9), aiming them as shown in the figure.
Note
The first screw to be started is the one shown by the arrow.
 
Tighten the screws (10) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
