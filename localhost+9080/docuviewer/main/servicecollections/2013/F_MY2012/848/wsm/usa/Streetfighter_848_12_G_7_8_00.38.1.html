Rear suspension
Important
Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Rear suspension system
The rear suspension uses a fully adjustable progressive pressurised hydraulic monoshock (10). The system consists of a rocker arm (8) and a linkage (14), which is fixed to the single-sided swinging arm.
The swinging arm pivots on the frame and the engine to achieve maximum rigidity; the rear end of the swinging arm supports an eccentric wheel hub which allows chain tension adjustment.
 
To adjust the rear shock absorber refer to Sect. D 4, Adjusting the rear shock absorber.
Removing the rear shock absorber
Undo screws (A) and remove heel guard (B).
To remove the rear shock absorber (10) from the swinging arm and from the rocker arm (8), remove the plug (C) and remove the upper screw (9) and the lower screw (13).
 
Overhauling the Streetfighter rear shock absorber
Before disassembling the shock absorber, measure the spring pre-load length (L). The same length must be restored upon reassembly.
Disassembling the Streetfighter rear shock absorber
Slacken the ring nut (A) and the spring adjuster ring nut (B) with a pin wrench or special tool.
Remove retaining ring (C) and the spring.
Visually inspect the shock absorber for oil leaks and other problems.
Replace the shock absorber if necessary.
Reassembling the Streetfighter rear shock absorber
Fit the spring into its seat in the shock absorber with the tapered end towards the lower mounting.
Fit the retaining ring (C) and the spring.
Screw in the spring adjuster nut (B) to obtain the previously measured length (L).
Note
One full turn of the adjuster nut changes the spring length by 1.5 mm.
 
Tighten down the lock ring nut (A) against the ring nut (B).
Removing the rear suspension rocker arm
Disconnect the top of the rear shock absorber from the rocker arm as described in “Removing the rear shock absorber” in this chapter.
Detach the linkage (14) from the rocker arm (8), loosening the screw (4), and recovering the bushes (11).
Before removing the rocker arm (8) from the frame, check the play of its internal needle roller bearings (7).
Grasp the rear end of the rocker arm and push and pull it in all directions.
If excessive play is encountered, the bearings need to be replaced.
To remove the rocker arm (8) from the frame, unscrew the nut (3) and keep the washer (2).
Slide out the screw (1) and remove the rocker arm assembly (8) from the frame.
Overhauling the rear suspension rocker arm
Disassembling the rear suspension rocker arm
The rocker arm movement is obtained by needle roller bearings (7) rotating on an inner spacer (5); two sealing rings (6) are placed at the outer ends and keep the lubricant inside the roller bearing cages.
Remove the inner spacer (5), the sealing rings (6), the needle roller bearings (7) using a suitable drift.
Important
Take care not to damage the bearing housings on the rocker arm while driving out the bearings. Once removed, the sealing rings (6) and needle roller bearings (7) may not be refitted.
Reassembling the rear suspension rocker arm
Insert a new needle roller bearing (7) on the drift no. 88713.1071, and lubricate with recommended grease.
Support the rocker arm and drive the needle roller bearings into the rocker arm bore until the tool seats against rocker arm.
Important
Introduce the needle roller bearings aligned with the hole to avoid any sticking: use a press, if necessary.
Insert one new sealing ring (7) (with the metallic side faced outwards) into the drift and bring it fully home on the previously mounted roller bearing.
Repeat the above procedure for the other needle roller bearing (7) and the other sealing ring (6).
Lubricate the bearings and seals with the recommended grease and insert the inner spacer (6).
Refitting the rear suspension
Lubricate the shank and thread of the screw (1) and the nut (3) underside with the recommended grease.
Insert the complete rocker arm assembly (8) between the frame supports.
Drive the screw (1) fully home against the frame.
On the opposite side, insert the nut (3) and the washer (2).
Tighten the nut (3) and the screw (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
 
Apply grease to thread and underside of screw (9) that secures the shock absorber upper part, and insert it in the rocker arm.
Position the shock absorber (10) with the reservoir at the top.
Insert the upper ball joint of the shock absorber, aligning it with the front hole in the rocker arm, and the lower ball joint inside the hole of the rear swinging arm.
Apply grease to the thread and the underside of the shock absorber lower retaining screw (13), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Position the plug (C) with the arrow facing up.
Install the bushes (11) on the linkage ball joints.
Insert linkage (14) lower end inside the swinging arm.
Grease the thread, underside and sliding surface of the lower retaining screw (12) and insert it in the swinging arm from the right-hand side. Tighten the screw (12) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Apply grease to the threads and underside of screws (4) that secure the linkage, and insert them in the rocker arm.
Insert the upper end of the linkage in the rocker arm and tighten the screw (4) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the heel guard (B) starting and tightening the screws (A) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
