FRONT AND REAR WHEELS

Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Removing the rear wheel
Operations
Section reference
Remove the exhaust silencers
L 8, Removing the exhaust system
Place the motorcycle on the rear service stand and engage the 1st gear.
Remove the clip (6).
Using a suitable socket wrench, loosen the wheel nut (1).
 
Fully unscrew the nut (1), then slide off washer (2) and bush (3).
Remove the rear wheel from the motorcycle.
Overhauling the rear wheel
Inspect the condition of the rear wheel rim.
As the wheel rim has no bearings, it should be supported using the service tool no. 88713.2951.
Note
This service tool can also be used to install the wheel on a balancing machine.
Overhauling the wheel rim
Inspect the wheel as described below.
Visually inspect the wheel for cracks, scoring and deformation: change the wheel rim if damaged.
Fit the wheel rim on service tool no. 88713.2951 using the original fasteners.
Using a dial gauge, appropriately supported, measure wheel rim run-out and out-of-round according to the wheel shaft axis (Sect. C 1.1, Rear wheel).
If the values measured are not within the tolerance limits, replace the wheel.
Refitting the rear wheel
Clean the mating surfaces and lubricate the threaded end of the wheel shaft with recommended grease.
Fit the wheel, and centre it on shaft and driving pins.
Fit bush (3), washer (2), and insert nut (1) by hand.
Tighten nut (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) checking that the hole on the nut is aligned with one of those on the wheel shaft.
Fit the clip (6), inserting the end in one of the holes in the shaft, orienting it as shown in the figure.
