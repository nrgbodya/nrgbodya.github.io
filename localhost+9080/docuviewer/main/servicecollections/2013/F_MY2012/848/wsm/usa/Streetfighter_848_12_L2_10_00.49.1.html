FUEL TANK
FUEL SYSTEM
Important
Bold reference numbers in this section identify parts not shown in the figures alongside the text, but which can be found in the exploded view diagram.
Removing the fuel tank
Remove the tank cover (2) from the tank (1) by loosening the screws (3).
Remove the seat and the side body panels (Sect. E 3, Removing the seat - Side Body Panel and Tail Guard Removal).
Remove the screw (13) and recover the washer (12).
Release the hoses (31) and (32) from the plates (6) and (7), respectively.
Lift tank, and disconnect the fuel sensor (A) from the main wiring.
Disconnect the flange quick-release fittings (24).
To release tank front fittings from frame, slide tank (1) out on bike rear side.
Removing the tank filler cap
Open the filler cap.
Loosen the outer screws (9) securing the filler cap (8) to the tank (1).
Remove the complete filler cap.
Recover the sealing washer (36).
Refitting the tank filler cap
Duly grease the new sealing washer (36) and its seat on tank.
Refit the complete filler cap (8) inside its seat on tank (1), and tighten the screws (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Replacing the tank flange
Loosen the six screws (21) securing the fuel tank flange. To lift the flange, remove three screws, and leave the other three ones in a cross pattern.
Remove the fuel pump (25) from the fuel tank.
Recover the sealing washer (26).
Undo and remove the two fixing screws (B) and move the protection (C).
Loosen the collars (27), and remove the fuel filter (28).
Before reassembly, carefully remove any deposits or scale from all parts.
Important
When installing the new filter, position it so that the arrow stamped on the outer casing points towards the flange.
Note
The flange is supplied as a spare part complete with the fuel pump and pressure regulator: the entire flange assembly must be replaced in the event of malfunction.
Refitting the fuel tank flange
Duly grease the sealing washer (26), position the flange, and refit by reversing the steps of the removal procedure.
Warning
Orientate the fuel pump (25) with the arrows (D) facing the front part of the tank (1).
 
Tighten the screws (21) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values), applying sequence 1-2-3-4-5-6, as shown in the figure.
Refitting the fuel tank
If the fuel tank has been disassembled, reposition all the parts as shown in the exploded view. In particular:
-
apply the recommended threadlocker to the screws (4) and (5).
-
Tighten the screws (4), (5) and union (16) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Make sure that the sound-deadening panel (14) is correctly positioned on the fuel tank, as shown in the figure.
To position tank (1), insert the front connectors on their seats on frame.
Connect the quick-release fittings (24) to the flange.
Connect the fuel sensor (A) to the main wiring harness.
Insert the hoses (31) and (32) inside the plates (6) and (7), respectively.
Secure the tank to the frame with screw (13) and washer (12).
Tighten screw (13) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
The USA version features the cover kit (37).
When reassembling the USA cover kit, position plates (F) by starting screws (E).
Place two special pads (10 mm thick) between plates (F) and tank (1).
 
Drive plates (F) fully home on the two pads.
Apply recommended threadlocker to the screws (E), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values), keeping plates (F) fully against the pads.
Remove pads.
If not fitted, insert the clips (G) on the plates (F), aiming them as shown in the figure.
Fit the switch cover (H) on the ignition switch, aiming it as shown in the figure, and keeping the edge (L) up.
Fit cover (2), by tightening the screws (3) to the specified torque, in a X-X-Y-Y sequence.
For the non-USA versions, refit the tank cover (2) on tank (1) starting and tightening the screws (3) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) in a crossed pattern sequence.
