Removing the rear brake control
Loosen and remove the pin (5) securing the gearchange pedal (1), and recover the O-rings (6) and the washer (16).
Undo and remove the screw (13) securing the gearchange lever to the gear selector shaft.
Withdraw the lever (14) complete with the gearchange control assembly.
Note
Mark the position of lever (14) relative to the gear selector shaft.
 
To remove lever (14) from gearchange control assembly, loosen screw (17) and recover nut (15), then slide out lever (14).
Disassembling the gearchange control
Refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this section for indications on disassembly and replacement of gearchange control components.
If the bush (2) inside the pedal (1) needs replacing, grease the external surface and drive the new bush into place using a press. The bush must be seated 2 mm below the outer face of the pedal.
Warning
After working on the gearchange control, check the position of the gearchange pedal.
To adjust the gearchange pedal position, follow the instructions indicated in Sect. D 4, Adjusting the position of the gear change and rear brake pedals.
Refitting the gearchange mechanism
Apply grease on the internal surface of the bush (2) and threadlocker on the pin (5).
Install O-ring (6) onto pin (5).
Insert the pin (5) in the gearchange lever (2), and insert the other O-ring (6) and the washer (16).
Fit the gearchange lever (2) on the footpeg plate inserting and tightening the pin (5) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit lever (14) on gearchange control, starting screw (17) and nut (15).
Tighten the nut (15) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) while holding screw (17).
Fit the gear control unit inserting the lever (14) on the gear control pin, apply threadlocker on the screw (13) and insert it on the lever (14).
Tighten the screw (13) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
