Undo the screw (17) and remove the oil breather valve (1) with the O-rings (2) from the engine. Check the condition of O-rings (2) and replace them if necessary.
Unscrew and remove the oil inlet and outlet nipples (8) from the clutch side crankcase half and recover the gaskets (27).
Unscrew and remove the oil filter support nipple (6) and remove also by-pass spring (7).
Remove the mesh filter (A) from the clutch-side crankcase half (Sect. D 4, Changing the coolant).
Remove the drain plug (10) with its seal (9).
Remove the neutral switch (4) with the sealing washer (3).
Remove the cylinder head stud bolts (5) with the aid of the appropriate tool.
To remove the engine sensor (23), unscrew the screw (25) and recover the washer (26).
Check the condition of O-rings (22) and replace them if necessary.
Refitting the external components
Make sure that the O-rings (22) are fitted on the crankcase.
Fit the engine sensor (23) in its seat in the crankcase half.
Insert the screw (25) with the washer (26) and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Use a feeler gauge to check the clearance between the engine sensor (23) and the timing gear (13). The value must be between 0.6 and 0.8 mm.
Check the condition of O-rings (2) and replace if necessary.
Install the oil vapour breather valve (1) in the crankcase along with O-rings (2), previously lubricated.
Tighten the screw (17) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
The aluminium gaskets (27) must be installed on the nipples (8), on the side of the nipple with the smaller diameter thread: position the gaskets so that the side with the square edge is facing the crankcase half. Apply the recommended threadlocker to the smaller diameter thread of nipples (8).
Tighten the nipples (8) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Position the by-pass spring (7) on the nipple (6) and apply the recommended threadlocker to the end of the nipple to be screwed in the crankcase half. Screw oil filter cartridge fitting (6) fully into the crankcase half, positioning the spring in the seat between the two notches (A). Tighten the nipple to the specified torque (Sect. C 3 , Engine tightening torque values).
Fit the seal (9) on the oil drain plug (10). Position the seal so that the side with the square edge (B) is facing the chain side crankcase half.
Clean the plug thread, apply a bead of THREE BOND TB1215 along the plug thread spreading it by the entire circumference (360Â°).
Screw the drain plug (10) with seal (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
 Note
After tightening, remove any excessive sealant.
Refit the neutral switch (4) with sealing washer (3).
Refit the mesh filter (A) (Sect. D 4, Changing the engine oil and filter cartridge).
Check the condition of the O-rings (28) of the stud bolts (5) and replace them if necessary.
Use tool (C) to fit the O-rings (28) in the corresponding seats (D) of the stud bolts (5).
Now fit the stud bolts (5) in the crankcase halves, applying sealant on the thread and tightening to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values). Use the appropriate commercial tool for this operation.
If it was removed, apply the recommended threadlocker to the dowel (24), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values), fit the sealing washer (31) on the service plug (32): the sealing washer must be oriented so that the square edge faces the clutch-side crankcase half. Apply prescribed threadlocker on the plug thread (32), insert it in the crankcase half and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).

N 8, Removing the generator cover
Slide out driving gear (A) of timing gear pair (13) and remove the key (14).
Straighten out the lockwasher (12) of nut (11).
Restrain the timing gear by inserting a pin in one of the holes, and unscrew the locking nut (11).
Remove the nut (11), washer (12), driven gear (B) and key (15) from the timing layshaft.
Refitting the timing gears
Before reassembling the removed parts, check timing gears (13) for wear. Change, if necessary.
Important
The timing gears (13) must always be replaced as a pair.
 
Refitting is the reverse of removal.
Warning
When introducing the driven gear (B) check that the key (15) is correctly fitted on the timing layshaft, align the gear slot with the key matching the driving gear timing mark (A) with the gear (B).
Important
On completion of the refitting operations, check that washer (12) is staked against nut (11) in such a way to prevent the nut from working loose.
