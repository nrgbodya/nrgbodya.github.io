Removing the rear brake calliper
Important
The brake manufacturer advises against any servicing of the internal components of brake callipers or the master cylinder. Incor­rect overhaul of these critical safety components can endanger rider and passenger safety.
 
Before removing the concerned parts, you must first carry out the following operations:

Remove the hose (A).
Undo the two fixing screws (8) securing the rear brake calliper (11) to the calliper holder bracket, and remove the brake calliper (11).
If it is necessary to remove the calliper holder bracket (B), refer to Sect. G 5, Removing the rear wheel eccentric hub and the rear wheel shaft.
Note
To replace the brake pads follow instructions in the paragraph “Checking brake pad wear and replacing brake pads” (Sect. D 4).
 
To remove the speed sensor (6), undo the fixing screw (7) paying attention to the spacer (5).
Only the following parts should be replaced:
-
calliper: pads, fasteners and bleed valve assembly;
-
master cylinder: control pedal, bleed valve assembly, reservoir and relevant components.
 
Refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this section for indications on replacement of the above components.
Removing the rear brake disc
Remove the rear eccentric hub (Sect. G 5, Removing the rear wheel eccentric hub and the rear wheel shaft).
Undo and remove the four fixing screws (1) of the brake disc to the wheel shaft, and remove the rear brake disc (4).
The brake disc must be perfectly clean, with no rust, oil, grease or other dirt and no deep scoring.
To check the wear limit of the brake disc, refer to Sect. C 1.1, Hydraulic brakes.
Refit the rear brake disc (4), and tighten the screws (1) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) in a sequence 1-2-3-4.
 
Refit the rear eccentric hub as described in Sect. G 5, Refitting the rear wheel eccentric hub and rear wheel shaft.
Refitting the rear brake calliper
If the brake hoses are replaced or if one of the rear brake system components has been removed, make sure that the hose con­nection unions on the master cylinder and the calliper are correctly positioned.
Warning
If incorrectly positioned, the hose can affect brake operation and foul moving parts. Position the hose as shown in the figure.
Remember to fit the copper sealing washers (12) to the hose end union when reconnecting the brake hose to the brake calliper (11).
After orientating the union, tighten the special screw (13) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
If the speed sensor (6) was removed, fit it on the calliper holder bracket with the spacer (5) and tighten the screw (7) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
 Note
The gap between sensor (6) and the brake disc fixing screw must be within 0.6 and 2.2  mm.
 
If it is necessary to remove the calliper holder bracket (B) in order to refit it, see Sect. G 5 - , Refitting the rear wheel eccentric hub and rear wheel shaft.
Insert the rear brake calliper (11) on the brake disc, aligning it with the holes of the calliper mounting bracket (B).
Grease the screws (8) and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
