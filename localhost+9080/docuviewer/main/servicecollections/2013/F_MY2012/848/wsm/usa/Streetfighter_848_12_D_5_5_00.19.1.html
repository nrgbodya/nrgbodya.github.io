Diagnosis instruments
DDS 2 (Ducati Diagnosis System 2.0) diagnosis instrument.
The "DDS 2" diagnosis instrument follows the operator, step by step, during the different diagnosis methods, with special descriptions and
documents on bike parts, electronic system wiring diagrams, and information about component
location.
The following maintenance operations can be carried out with the "DDS 2" (Ducati Diagnosis System 2.0) diagnosis instrument:
-
check and adjustment of timing belt tension;
-
reset of the throttle potentiometer sensor (TPS);
-
adjustment of the idle speed;
-
check of engine oil pressure;
-
check of engine cylinder compression;
-
check of fuel pressure;
-
immobilizer override procedure;
-
check of battery charging system.
Checking and adjusting timing belt tension
To check timing belt tension, use the DDS 2 instrument and the belt tensioning kit.
Position the crankshaft so that the piston of the horizontal cylinder is at TDC of its power stroke.
This is achieved by aligning the timing mark (C) of the timing driveshaft pulley (19) with the reference notch (D) on the clutch cover (20).
Measure the horizontal timing belt on the section (21).
Before measuring the vertical timing belt, turn the crankshaft by 270° in the engine rotation direction (vertical cylinder TDC, in the combustion stroke), and repeat the procedure used for the horizontal cylinder, measuring the voltage on branch (25) of the vertical belt.
The tension values are those detailed in Sect. C 1.1, Timing system/valves and must be checked when the engine is cold: assembly values must be applied when installing a new belt, while service values must be applied when belt tension reaches 70 Hz.
Warning
The timing belts can become slack during normal operation. When checking belt tension, if the reading is less than 70 Hz, restore to the specified values (Sect. C 1.1, Timing system/valves).
 
If the tension value is incorrect, increase or reduce belt tension, moving the adjustable mobile tensioner (23), loosening the nut (24).
Tighten the nut (24) securing the tensioner pulley.
Recheck the timing belt tension.
Repeat the above procedure until the correct belt tension is obtained.
Once the desired tension is reached, tighten the fixing nut (24) of the mobile tensioner (23) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Resetting the Throttle Potentiometer Sensor (TPS)
The linear throttle potentiometer sensor has no potentiometer adjustments, the following procedure shall thus be applied to ensure a correct system operation.
Warning
This procedure must precede the first start-up of the vehicle after replacement of the throttle body, potentiometer, ECU, or wiring.
 
Make sure that the throttle valve is seated against the stop screw (throttle twistgrip released).
In this position the ECU learns the position sensor angle. The DDS diagnosis instrument is necessary to carry out this operation.
Important
The following screens are given just as an example, and thus do not exactly refer to the model described in this manual.
 
Select the Ducati model, engine type, and model year.
From the self-diagnosis menu, select the type of engine electronic control unit: OEM or Performance.
Connect the interface wiring to the bike diagnosis socket, and the two VCI interface power clamps to the battery. Connect the remaining connector to the VCI (Vehicle Communication Interface) module.
Switch on the instrument panel.
Select the ADJUSTMENTS menu on the left column, then select "TPS Reset", then press ADJUST.
Press "CONFIRM" and make sure that engine is off, then press "CONFIRM" again.
Should the engine be ON, a message will ask you to turn it off.
If the operation has been completed successfully, the corresponding message will be displayed. If not, an error message will appear, and the procedure will have to be repeated making sure that the conditions necessary for the TPS reset function to be successfully executed are present.
 
Adjusting the idle speed
 Note
a
Check that the bike is provided with electronic control unit, original intake and exhaust systems, otherwise fit original components.
b
The temperature inside the premises where the adjustment is carried out shall be above 16 °C.
 
Connect the inserts of the exhaust gas analyser no. 88713.1010 to the outlets on the exhaust pipes using the unions (19).
Make sure that the horizontal throttle valve pulley is seated against the anti-stitching screw when the throttle twistgrip is fully closed.
Make sure that the throttle cable free play is correctly adjusted.
Reset the TPS (throttle potentiometer).
Check:
-
that throttle angle reading with engine off is 2.6° ±0.1° and, if not, repeat the "Throttle potentiometer sensor (TPS) reset" procedure;
-
that the idle speed is detected, namely that the "closed throttle" parameter is ON; if not, repeat the TPS reset procedure.
 
Start the engine and wait for the coolant temperature to reach 75 °C.
When coolant temperature exceeds 75 °C, start the following adjustment operations, bearing always in mind that coolant temperature shall not exceed 90 °C at the end of the adjustment procedure.
Warning
If the engine is shut off before proceeding with adjustment of the CO level, wait one minute after restarting for the enrichment phase to finish (Crank time).
 
Check that the CO level on both cylinders is between 0.4% and 1.4%.
If so, check engine speed.
 
Check of detected engine average speed. The following options are possible:
A
if the average reading is between 1500 and 1600 rpm (1550±50 rpm), the adjustment procedure is completed;
B
if engine speed is averagely below 1500 rpm, open both by-pass screws (20) by 1/8 turn at a time, until the above value is reached. Should engine speed be averagely above 1600 rpm, make sure that there is no anomalous air infiltration downstream the throttles, cracks or cuts on intake manifolds and stepper pipes, etc...
 
Warning
Anyway, the by-pass screws of both cylinders shall not be open more than 2.5 turns from the fully closed position.
Actions to be taken in case idle speed adjustment has not been successfully completed.
1
Repeat the entire idle speed adjustment procedure.
2
Check the CO level on both cylinders: it shall be automatically kept within the 0.4%-1.4% range by the engine electronic management system:
-
if this check is not successful replace the Lambda sensor and/or inspect the cylinders (clearance, strokes, valve sealing, etc.).
-
repeat the entire idle speed adjustment procedure.
3
check the vacuum pressures downstream of the throttle valve (carry out this check only after completing the steps described above).
Warning
Idle speed adjustment takes priority over balancing the vacuum pressures downstream of the throttle valve. Differences in pressure between the horizontal and vertical cylinder are therefore acceptable, provided that the idle speed is adjusted correctly.
 
-
If you are unable to attain the desired idle speed setting, try reducing the vacuum differential downstream of the throttle valve.
-
repeat the entire idle speed adjustment procedure.
Checking the engine oil pressure
To carry out this procedure a commercial engine oil pressure tester can be used.
 
Oil pressure test values:
Warm engine
(Minimum oil temperature = 80 °C)
1100÷1300 rpm
greater than 0.8 bar.
3500÷4000 rpm
greater than 4 bar.
Important
The maximum pressure must never exceed 6.0 bar.
 
Excessively high pressure may indicate that the relief valve is stuck. Excessively low pressure, on the other hand, may be caused by the relief valve being stuck in the open position, the spring being too weak, or a faulty oil pump. Other causes are excessively worn seals and gaskets or a badly worn engine.
Cylinder compression test
Engine performance is directly correlated to the pressure that can be measured in the combustion chambers of the two cylinders. Pressure which is too high/low or an excessive difference between the two cylinders will cause a drop in engine performance and can cause engine breakdown.
 
Check the pressure in each cylinder:
-
standard value: 11÷12 bar;
-
min. value: 10 bar;
-
maximum permissible difference between cylinders: 2 bar.
 
An excessively high pressure value can be caused by:
-
build up of deposits in the combustion chamber.
An excessively low pressure value can be caused by:
-
gas escaping between the cylinder head and the barrel;
-
worn valve seats;
-
bent valve stems;
-
incorrect valve clearances;
-
worn cylinder or piston rings.
Fuel pressure test
The maximum pressure must be equal 3 bar (nominal).
 
Testing the battery charging system
 
You can determine the engine rpm required for generator to produce enough current to charge battery, feed the injection ignition system and all electric items fitted to motorcycle. The clamp-type ammeter detects the magnetic field generated by the current passing through that cable.
Warning
The clamp-type ammeter must not be connected to wires through which electrical current is flowing.
 
Insert the clamp-type ammeter into the battery positive terminal lead with the arrow on the clamp pointing towards the battery positive terminal (+).
 
If the measured current is a positive quantity, it means that generator is feeding all electric items and charging battery at the same time. If the current has a negative sign, this means that the charging system is not able to power the electrical loads and a significant amount of the current required must be supplied by the battery, which is therefore discharging.
Important
If polarity is reversed when clamping the ammeter onto the cable, the sign of the readings will also be reversed, giving rise to incorrect diagnosis.
