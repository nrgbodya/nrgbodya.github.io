Loosen and remove the water pump cover (12) five fixing screws (13) to the generator cover (16).
Remove the water pump cover (12).
Clean the pump housing from any scale. Check the bearing wear by turning the impeller shaft (10); in case of excessive clearance, it is necessary to replace them as follows.
Remove the snap ring (3) from the impeller shaft, and the washer (4) underneath.
Slide off the impeller (10) with the sealing ring (9) from the outside.
Recover the ceramic washer (A) and the seal ring (B) positioned on the internal side.
Undo and remove the screws (1) with the washer (2).
Working from the impeller side, use a suitable drift to press on the inner ring of end bearing (5) until it can be extracted from the cover.
Use the same technique to remove the other bearing (6).
Working from the inside of the cover, remove the counter-washer (8) of the mechanic seal (9).
Remove the inner spacer (7).
Check the condition of the mechanical seal components: there should be no signs of deformation, cracking, or excessive wear. In the case of damage, both components must be replaced.
Refitting the water pump
Clean the seat in the cover, any parts you intend to reuse, and the impeller shaft. Lubricate with engine oil and refit as follows.
Using drift no. 88713.0869 fit the new mechanic seal (9) on the impeller shaft.
Working on cover internal side, insert the inner spacer (7) with the sharp edge (A) outwards.
Using a suitable drift working on the outer ring, drive bearing (6) - with the shielded side (B) facing the cover - fully home on the spacer (7).
Fit the other bearing (5), driving it fully home on the previous one.
Fit the two screws (1) with the washers (2), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
 
 
Turn the cover upside down and, using drift part no. 88713.0870, drive the counter-washer (8) - aimed as shown in the figure - fully home on the spacer (7).
Fit the other seal ring (B) and the ceramic washer (A).
Fit the impeller shaft (10) with the mechanical seal (9), and turn the cover upside down.
Insert the spacer (4), aimed as shown in the figure, and lock in place with the snap ring (3).
Turn the impeller (10) and make sure that it can rotate freely, without excessive resistance.
Clean the mating surface thoroughly on the pump cover and on the generator cover.
Apply a bead of sealing compound to the water pump cover (12), as shown in the figure.
Fit the centring dowels (11) and position the cover (12) on the generator cover.
Fit the cover retaining screws (13).
Tighten the screws (13) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Refit the components removed in the procedure.
