Adjusting the steering head bearing clearance
Note
To adjust the steering bearing clearance, follow what is described in Sect. D 4, Adjusting the steering head bearing play.
 
If the problems found are not solved, check the wear of steering bearings (3) and replace them, if necessary, as described in paragraph “Removing the steering head components” of this chapter.
Steering angle adjustment
Loosen the nuts (9).
Loosen the adjusting dowels (8) on both sides of the bottom yoke.
Use a 6 to 6.5 mm spacer (A) fitted to the fork sleeve, or use a gauge.
Turn the front forks to the right until the spacer (A) is seated against the frame top tube.
Turn the adjuster dowel (8) on the opposite side to bring it into contact with the retainer on the steering head.
Apply threadlocker to the thread of nut (9).
Hold the adjuster dowel (8) firm and tighten the nut (9).
Turn the forks to the opposite side: and repeat the process to adjust the other adjuster dowel and tighten the relative nut.

Note
All parts fitted to the steering head and bottom yoke, including wiring and hoses, can remain on the motorcycle provided they do not hinder the following operations.
 
Loosen the screw (6) securing the steering head (7) to the ring nut (4).
Remove the steering head (7).
With the service tool no. 88713.1058 loosen the ring nut (4) and unscrew it from the steering shaft.
Slide the sealing ring (2), the inner ring (A) and the ball race (B) of the upper bearing (3) off the steering shaft.
Remove the bottom yoke (1) complete with the steering shaft from the frame tube.
Remove the ball race (B) of the lower bearing (3).
The inner race (A) of the lower bearing (3) and the relative sealing ring (2) will remain on the steering shaft.
Using a universal puller (see figure), remove the inner ring (A) from the steering shaft, taking care not to damage the seat.
Important
Once removed, the sealing rings (2) and the bearings (3) must not be refitted.
 
Using a suitable punch, remove the outer bearing races (C) from the steering tube. Proceed with extreme care to avoid damaging the seats.
Refitting the steering tube components
Important
The steering tube bearings (3) are identical but in no case may their components be swapped around during reassembly.
 
Clean all contact surfaces and lubricate with the recommended grease.
To fit the outer rings (C) of bearings (3) to the steering tube, use tool no. 88713.1062. Proceed as follows:
-
heat the steering tube to 150 °C;
-
fit the outer rings (C) in their seats on the steering tube;
-
fit the fixed bush (E) with threaded hole of the tool into the lower race;
-
fit the other movable bush (F) into the upper end of the tool and drive it fully home against the upper bearing race;
-
tighten the nut (G), and use a wrench to seat the outer rings (C) fully in the steering tube;
-
leave the tool assembled until the steering tube has cooled down to ensure that bearings are properly seated.
Insert the sealing ring (2) (with the rim facing upwards) and the inner ring (A) of the bottom bearing (3) onto the steering shaft (D) after heating it for about 10 minutes to 120°C.
Insert drift no. 88713.1072 onto the steering shaft. Push the inner race (A) on the sealing ring (2), manually pushing for at least 10-15 seconds.
Lubricate the inner bearing internal race (A) with the recommended grease.
Fit the ball race (B) on the steering shaft (D) with the smaller diameter of the cage facing upwards, and grease the ball race.
Insert the steering shaft into the steering tube, and push it in until it is axially seated.
Fit the bottom yoke assembly to frame.
Grease the ball race (B) and fit it to the upper outer ring (C) of the frame.
Fit the inner race (A) of the upper bearing (3) to the steering head, with the larger diameter side of the race facing upwards.
Install the sealing ring (2) with the flat side facing upwards.
Tighten the steering ring nut (4) by hand until it seats against the sealing ring (2).
Fit special bush no. 88713.1058 to the ring nut (4) and apply the torque wrench to the bush.
Tighten the ring nut (4) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Fit the steering head (7) on the ring nut (4), aligning the fork leg bores with the corresponding bores on the bottom yoke. Refit the fork legs (Sect. G 2, Refitting the front forks).
Apply recommended grease to the screw (6).
Tighten the screw (6) on the steering head to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the ignition switch (Sect. P 8, Refitting the immobiliser).
Refit the tank cover (M), and tighten the screws (L) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
