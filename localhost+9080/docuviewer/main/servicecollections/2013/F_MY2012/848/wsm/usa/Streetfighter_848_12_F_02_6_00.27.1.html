Removing the clutch master cylinder assembly
Warning
The clutch master cylinder manufacturer advises against servicing of the clutch master cylinder (1) due to the safety-critical nature of this component. Incorrect overhaul of this component could endanger rider safety.
 
Replacement operations shall be limited to the following parts: control lever, reservoir assembly, and master cylinder fasteners.
 
Operations
Section reference
Drain the clutch system
D 4, Draining the clutch hydraulic circuit
Remove the LH rear-view mirror
E 1, Removing the rear-view mirrors
Unscrew the special screw (16) recovering the sealing washers (17), to release the clutch master cylinder unit (1) from the clutch control pipe (6).
Undo the two clamp screws (19) securing the clutch master cylinder assembly to the handlebar.
Refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this section for indications on disassembly and replacement of the master cylinder components.
Refitting the clutch master cylinder assembly
Insert the clutch master cylinder unit (1) on the left side of the handlebar to bring the terminal internal edge in correspondence to the mark (A).
Tighten the fixing screws (19) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) following the sequence 1-2-1 starting from the upper screw.
Locate the hose (6) and sealing washers (17) on the master cylinder unit (1) and secure with the special screw (16), without tightening.
Warning
An incorrectly positioned hose can cause clutch faults and interfere with moving parts.
For the positioning of the clutch hose (6) and retaining clips, see the illustration at the end of this section.
Orientate the hose (6) as shown in the figure and then tighten the special screw (16) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).

Removing the clutch slave cylinder
Warning
The clutch slave cylinder manufacturer advises against servicing of internal components of clutch slave cylinder (23) due to the safety critical nature of this component.
Incorrect overhaul can endanger rider and passenger safety.
The replacement operations shall be limited to the following components: clutch slave cylinder, bleed valve, seals and complete clutch pushrod assembly.
 
Operations
Section reference
Drain the clutch system
D 4, Filling the clutch circuit
Undo the screws (24) and slide out the clutch slave cylinder (23).
Remove dust cap (4) and bleed valve (3), and undo screw (5), taking care not to damage sealing washers (17): unit (23) has been released from hose (6).
Push the piston to force out all the fluid from inside the cap.
At this point, it is possible to slide also the clutch pushrod (27).
Reassembling the clutch slave cylinder
Position hose (6) on the clutch slave cylinder (23).
Position the two sealing washers (17) and tighten the screw (5) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refit the bleed valve (3) and the dust cap (4).
To position the pipe retaining clamps (6) refer to the table on the following page.
Push the internal piston (A) to force out all the fluid from inside the cap.
Make sure the anti-rotation pin (30) is fitted on the clutch pushrod (27).
Proceed as follows in case the anti-rotation pin (30) has been removed from the clutch pushrod (27).
Turn the clutch pushrod (27) until the axis of the anti-rotation pin (30) positioning hole is horizontal, as shown in the figure;
Grease the anti-rotation pin (30), and insert it into the clutch pushrod (27) hole.
Insert the anti-rotation insert (25) fully home into the clutch pushrod (27) by matching the anti-rotation pin (30) with the slots (B) on the insert (25).
Note
The tab (C) of insert (25) must be inwards (casing side).
Turn the clutch pushrod (27) counter clockwise until the hole axis of the anti-rotation pin (30) is aligned with the centreline of the casing cover machined surface (D), as shown in the figure.
Insert the clutch slave cylinder (23) into the pushrod (27) and bring it fully home on the anti-rotation insert (25).
Note
While inserting the clutch slave cylinder (23), ensure that tooth (C)
on insert (25) matches with slot (E) on cylinder.
 
Fix the clutch slave cylinder (23) by starting the screws (24).
Note
To bring the clutch slave cylinder (6) internal surface near the casing cover as uniformly as possible, screw and tighten the screws (24) alternatively.
Tighten the screws (24) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values), in the sequence 1 - 2 - 3 - 1.
