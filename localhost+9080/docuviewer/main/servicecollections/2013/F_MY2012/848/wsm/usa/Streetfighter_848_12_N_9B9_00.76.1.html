Overhauling the crankcase halves
Visually inspect the engine crankcase halves.
Check that the surfaces of the crankcase halves are perfectly flat using a reference surface.
Check that the bearings (1) and (18), and the bushes (2) and (17) are in good conditions. Note that the main bearings must always be changed in pairs (refer to the procedure below in “Main bearings”).
Note
When overhauling an engine it is good practice to replace all the crankcase half bearings.
After having replaced the gearbox shaft bearings (28) and (9), secure them in the crankcase half with the spacers (21) and the retaining plate (10).
At each overhaul it is recommended to replace also the sealing ring (8) on the outside of bearing (9).
Replace the bearings (7) and (23) on the ends of the gearbox shafts and timing layshaft bearings (4) and (15).
Take care not to lose the shim (6) interposed between the bearing (7) on the end of the gearbox primary shaft and the chain side crankcase half.
The sealing ring (14) on the external side of the needle roller bearing (15) must be replaced at each overhaul.
Check that the oilways are free of restrictions or clogging. Check the condition of centring bushes (22). If apparently distorted or loose in their seats, change them using proper tools. When the centring bushes (22) are hard to remove from casing, use a left-hand tap to force bushes out.
Important
The bushes (22) must always be replaced when they have been removed using the above procedure.
Remove the bearing (23), the circlip (5) and the needle bearing (4).
Main bearings
The main bearings are of the angular contact type with offset inner races so that the balls transmit loads from one groove to the other along straight lines at a certain angle to the bearing axis. The angle-contact ball bearings are designed for bearing combined loading (radial-axial loads).
Bearings of this type can bear direct axial loads in one direction only. In fact, under the action of a radial load inside the bearing, an axial force is created that must be counterbalanced by an axial force acting in the opposite direction: that is why these bearings are generally fitted back to back in pairs.
 Note
The supplied main bearings can be bearings with bush (A) or flanged bearings (B) with bush integrated in the external race of the bearing.
 
To replace the bearings proceed as follows:
-
heat the crankcase in an oven to 100 °C;
-
remove the bearing using a drift and hammer;
-
install the new bearing (while the crankcase is still hot) keeping it perfectly square in its seat using a tubular drift that only bears on the outer ring of the bearing;
-
allow the parts to cool and check that the bearing is securely seated in the crankcase half.
Bearings with bush
Important
On worn engines, the bearing holder bushes may no longer be tight fitted in the crankcase halves.
 
After having removed the bush, check that the interference fit between the crankcase and the bush, with bearing fitted, is not less than 0.03 mm, otherwise, replace the crankcase halves.
 Note
Fit the bearings (1) and (18) orienting them so that the side with the “letter” (C) is facing the bushes (2) and (17).
Flanged bearings
Important
On badly worn engines, bearing outer rings may have developed clearance in the crankcase halves - normally, bearing outer rings are interference-fit in the casing.
 
Check that the interference fit between the crankcase and the bearings (1) and (18) installed, is not less than 0.03 mm, otherwise, replace the crankcase halves.
Important
After installing new main bearings, shim the crankshaft as described in the paragraph “Shimming the shafts” and “Reassembling the crankcase halves" of this section.
Reassembling the crankcase halves
The crankcase halves must be in good condition and perfectly clean. The mating surfaces must be perfectly flat and free from burrs.
Overhauling the clutch side crankcase half
The following parts must be present on the internal side of the crankcase half:
The secondary shaft bearing (23); apply grease on the bearing rollers. Fit the inner race (A) removed previously from the bearing. Apply grease to the inner race.
The primary shaft bearing (28), secured with the screws (20) and the retaining spacer (21); apply recommended threadlocker to the screws (20), and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
The main bearing (18) with the corresponding bush (17) (if present).
The needle bearing (15) with retaining circlip (16) installed in correspondence with the end of the timing layshaft.
Insert the circlip (5) in the crankcase half installing it in its seat on the bearing (4).
 
Refitting the generator-side crankcase half
The double race ball bearing (9) supporting the selector fork shaft shall be present on crankcase half inner side.
Apply threadlocker to the screws (11).
Fit the retaining plate (10) to the gearbox secondary shaft bearing (9) starting the screws (11) fully into the chain-side crankcase half.
Note
The bearing retaining plate must be positioned so that the hole countersunk side is facing upwards.
 
Tighten the screws (11) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
The primary shaft bearing (7) with the internal spacer (6), oriented so that the closed side of the plastic roller cage (M) faces the crankcase half: apply grease to the bearing rollers. Fit the inner race (A) removed previously from the bearing. Apply grease to the inner race.
Fit the main bearing (1) with the corresponding bush (2) (if present).
Fit the bearing (4) with the retaining circlip (5) installed in correspondence with the timing layshaft.
Insert the circlip (5) in the crankcase half installing it in its seat on the bearing (4).
Note
Bearings do not have a specific mounting position (except main bearings), however it is good practice to install the bearings so that side bearing with the writing is facing upwards.
 
Fit the mesh filter as described in Sect. D 4, Changing the engine oil and filter cartridge.
Important
To avoid damaging the filter, insert it by hand only. Do not use hammers or other metal tools.
 
Above the mesh filter seat there is a plug (19) which closes off the lubrication oilway. If it is to be replaced, apply the prescribed sealant to its thread.
Tighten the screws and the plug to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Shimming the shafts
Before assembling the crankcase halves, calculate the shims required to obtain the correct end float of the crankshaft and gearbox shafts.
To determine the correct shim thickness proceed as follows.
Shimming the crankshaft
After having installed the new main bearings (with bush (A) or flanged bearings (B)) proceed as follows to determine the total “SA” height of the shimming:
-
measure the distance “LA” between the bearing contact surfaces on the crankshaft;
-
measure the depths “LA1” and “LA2” corresponding to the distance between contact surface of the crankcase half and the contact surface of the bearing inner race.
 
Add 0.30 mm preload for crankshaft axial bearings to bed in correctly into their seats (inner race seated inside outer race).
Thus, we obtain:
SA=LA1+LA2+0.30-LA.
To calculate the thickness of each shim note that:
SA=SA1+SA2
where “SA1” and “SA2” represent the shims for the clutch-side crankcase half 1 and the generator-side crankcase half 2.
Considering the alignment of the shaft, this gives:
SA1=LA1+0.15-LA/2;
and then the second shimming: SA2=SA-SA1.
In addition to the above procedure, the following is a practical shimming method, providing a guide on how to calculate the crankshaft shim thickness accurately.
Install a shim of minimum thickness (1.90 mm) on each side of the crankshaft to prevent contact between the crankshaft web and the crankcase.
Install the crankshaft in the crankcase half and close the engine block.
Fit four M8 screws in the seats indicated in the figure and tighten them to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Place a dial gauge (C) with magnetic base on a support plate fixed to crankcase.
Bring the stylus into contact with the end of the crankshaft and set the dial gauge to zero in this position.
Place a lever (D) between crankcase and crank web of the crankshaft pushing towards dial gauge.
Note on the dial gauge the total clearance and add a preload of (0.30 mm) plus the thickness of the shims used (1.90x2=3.8 mm).
Divide the resulting value by two to obtain the thickness of the shim packs to be installed at either end of the crankshaft.
Note
After assembling the crankcase halves, the crankshaft should turn with some interference in the new bearings.

If removed, apply recommended threadlocker on the dowel (35) thread and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
If removed, apply recommended threadlocker on the plug (19) and tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Install the O-ring (32) in its seat (A) in the crankcase half. Fit the hose (33) in the crankcase half, inserting the end (B) fully in the O-ring. Align the hole (L) of the hose with the threaded hole (M) in the crankcase half. Apply threadlocker to the screw (34) and start it in the threaded hole (M). Tighten the screw to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Engine tightening torque values).
Install the timing layshaft (30) in the needle roller bearing (4) in the clutch-side crankcase half.
To avoid damaging the sealing ring (14) on the timing layshaft, protect the threaded end of the shaft with the special protective cap 88700.5749. Dampen sealing ring (14) with alcohol and fit it on the timing layshaft. Push seal until it contacts the roller bearing (4).
Fit the snap ring (29) in the groove on the shaft and remove the protective cap.
Note
When refitting used components, before installing the gearbox assembly in the crankcase half, make sure that the inner races (C) of the gearbox shaft bearings are fitted into the correct bearings and have not been left on the shafts.
 
Match gearbox shafts, fit the shims and fit them to the clutch-side crankcase half.
