Removing the front forks
Before removing the front forks, it is first necessary to remove the following parts:

Important
Not all the components described in this chapter are available as individual spare parts, however they are indicated for clearer explanation of the procedure.
 
Disconnect connection (A) of main wiring sensor.
Undo the screw (30), and remove sensor (31) with sealing washer (32).
Loosen the screws (25) securing the fork legs to the steering head (23).
Loosen the screws (24) securing the fork legs to the bottom yoke (22).
Withdraw the fork legs (2) and (1) downwards in order to carry out all the necessary overhaul operations.
Overhauling the front forks
Note
It is advisable to loosen the top cap (7) when the fork leg is still fitted onto the motorcycle.
Note
The specific tools for the overhaul of the fork, are described in Sect. C 4, Special tools for the frame.
 
Loosen the spring pre-load adjuster before unscrewing the plug (7).
Unscrew the top plug (7) complete with the rebound damping adjuster.
Disassembling the front fork
Restrain the counter nut (13) with a hex. wrench.
Using another hexagon wrench, unscrew and remove the plug (7), with its sealing gasket, from the cartridge (8).
Unscrew the counter nut (13) up to the final threads on the cartridge (8).
While restraining the spring (9) with one hand, withdraw the bush (20) sideways and then slowly release spring (9).
Slide off the spring (9).
Slide the pre-load tube (12).
Drain the oil from inside the leg by pumping with the stanchion (18) and the cartridge (8).
Warning
This movement generates pressure so that the oil will be rapidly expelled from the fork leg. Aim the jet of oil into a previously prepared container and avoid contact with fork oil.
 
Block the fork tube-calliper unit (6) with a vice provided with protection jaws.
Unscrew the compression damping adjuster (3).
Remove the adjuster screw (3) and recover sealing washer (4).
Withdraw the cartridge assembly (8).
Slip the dust seal (15) off the stanchion (18), prising it off with a screwdriver.
Remove the snap ring (17).
Important
Take care not to damage the fork tube - calliper unit (6) and the seat of snap ring (17) in the stanchion (18).
 
To separate the stanchion (18) from the fork tube - calliper unit (6), pull sharply outwards repeatedly to gradually displace the upper bush (26).
Insert the tip of a screwdriver in the slit in the bush (11) and open it up sufficiently to slide the bush off the slider.
Remove the following parts from the slider:
upper bush (26);
collar (27);
sealing ring (21);
snap ring (17);
dust seal (15).
Inspecting the front fork
Place the spring on a flat surface and measure the free length (L).
Service limit:
270 mm
Replace the spring if the length is not within the specified limit.
Inspect the outer surfaces of both sliders and the internal surfaces of both sleeves. They must be free from scoring, steps, or dents.
Check that the outer tubes are straight:
maximum allowed error 0.10 mm.
Whenever the slider is removed from the sleeve it is good practice to replace bushes (20) and (26).
Check the collar (27) for distortion in the area indicated. Replace if distorted.
Reassembling the front fork
Vice the fork tube - calliper unit.
Protect the end of the slider with tape.
Important
Before installation, smear the sliding edges of sealing ring (21) with fork oil or seal grease.
Install the sealing ring (21) with the marked surface facing the dust seal (15).
 
Install the following components in the fork tube - calliper unit (6):
-
dust seal (15);
-
snap ring (17);
-
sealing ring (21);
-
collar (27);
-
upper bush (26);
-
lower bush (11).
Note
Before assembling the fork tube - calliper unit with the stanchion, lubricate the bush sliding surfaces with fork oil.
 
Push the bush (26) and the collar (27) into the stanchion (18) using the sealing ring tool (F, part no. 88713.3204).
Drive the sealing ring (21) into the outer tube using the same tool.
Fit the snap ring (17) and the dust seal (15).
 
Important
The stanchion must freely slide on the fork tube - calliper unit. Only support the outer units and tubes with your hands to avoid damaging the sealing rings and the bushes.
 
Insert the cartridge assembly (8) in the fork tube - calliper unit (6), screwing the tool no. 88713.3203 onto the cartridge.
Fit the sealing washer (4), and the compression adjuster screw (3).
Pour in half of the prescribed quantity of oil in each fork leg (Sect. C 2, Fuel, lubricants and other fluids).
Pump with tool no. 88713.3203 to force the oil to fill internal volume completely.
Slide both the damping cartridge and outer sleeve fully down the inner tube.
Pour the remaining oil into the fork leg and measure the oil level.
Important
Keep the fork leg vertical when measuring the oil level. Make sure the oil level is the same in both fork legs.
 
Recommended oil:
SHELL ADVANCE FORK 7.5 or DONAX TA
Standard capacity:
0.480 dm3 for each fork leg
Standard oil level:
104 mm
 
The quantity of oil affects fork response during the final part of the compression stroke.
High oil level increases compression loading, low oil level will decrease it.
Clean off any oil from the spring (9) and the counter nut (13) before refitting.
 
Install the pre-load tube (12).
Insert the spring (9), bush (15) and counter nut (13), with the conical side facing counter nut (26).
Screw the counter nut (13) onto the cartridge (8) with a hexagon wrench.
Using the same wrench, restrain the counter nut and screw the top plug (7) complete with the O-ring onto the cartridge (8).
Tighten the upper plug (7) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Refitting the front forks
Refit the fork legs, positioning them at the height shown in the figure relative to the upper surface of the bottom yoke.
Position the fork legs (2) and (1).
Tighten to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) the screws (24) and (25) securing the fork legs to the bottom yoke (22) and to the steering head (23).
Important
If the screws (24) and (25) were removed upon disassembly, smear the threads with the specified grease before tightening.
 
Position sensor (31) with sealing washer (32) and tighten the screw (30) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
Connect connection (A) of main wiring sensor.
Warning
Do not use the motorcycle without the front mudguard fitted to avoid the risk of the brake hoses fouling the wheel upon braking.
