Removing the front brake master cylinder
Warning
The brake master cylinder manufacturer advises against servicing the brake master cylinder due to the safety critical nature of this component. Incorrect overhaul of these critical safety components can endanger rider and passenger safety.
Maintenance operations on these units are limited to renewal of the following parts: control lever, fluid reservoir assembly and relative fasteners and master cylinder fasteners.
 
Operations
Section reference
Drain the braking system
D 4, Changing the brake fluid
Remove the rear-view mirrors
E 1, Removing the rear-view mirrors
Undo the special screw (3), recovering the sealing washers (4) to release the front brake master cylinder unit (1) from the calliper master cylinder hose (19).
Undo the screws (9) securing the mounting U-bolt (8), and then remove the front brake master cylinder assembly (1) from the handlebar.
For disassembling the components of the master cylinder assembly (1), refer to the exploded view at the beginning of this chapter.
Refitting the front brake master cylinder
Insert the front brake master cylinder unit (1) on the right side of the handlebar to bring the terminal internal edge in correspondence to the mark (A).
Tighten the fixing screws (9) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values) following the sequence 1-2-1 starting from the upper screw.
Position the calliper master cylinder hose (19) and sealing washers (4) on the master cylinder unit (1), and secure with the special screw (3), without tightening.
Warning
An incorrectly positioned hose can cause clutch faults and interfere with moving parts.
 
Orientate the hose (19) as illustrated in the figure and then tighten the special screw (3) to the specified torque (Sect. C 3, Frame tightening torque values).
To position calliper master cylinder hose (19) and retaining clips, see the figure at the end on this section.
